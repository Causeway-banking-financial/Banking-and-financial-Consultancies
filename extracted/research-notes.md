## Sukuk Market Developments (Jan 2026)
Source: Arab News / Fitch Ratings (January 8, 2026)

Key Facts:
- Global sukuk issuance rose 25% in 2025, reaching approximately $300 billion
- Global outstanding sukuk surpassed $1 trillion by end of 2025
- Sukuk's share of debt capital markets: GCC 41%, ASEAN 16%, Turkiye 8%
- 82.5% of rated sukuk are investment grade
- 90.5% of issuers carry Stable Outlooks
- No sukuk defaults recorded over the past four years
- Frontier markets (Egypt, Jordan, Sri Lanka) tapped sukuk market in 2025
- Algeria, Tunisia, Malta, Philippines issued sukuk rules in 2025

Quote: "We expect global sukuk issuance to sustain momentum in 2026, with continued growth in the core markets and a rising share in EMs — at about 16 percent of all US dollar debt capital market issuance in 2025 excluding China." — Bashar Al-Natoor, Fitch's global head of Islamic Finance

## Yemen Economy Updates (Jan 2026)
Sources: Reuters, UN, IMF, World Bank

Key Facts:
- Yemen humanitarian crisis worsening in 2026 due to funding cuts
- Aid needs rise to 21 million people
- 2025: Only $680 million afforded to UN (28% of target)
- Central Bank of Yemen denied issuing new currency (Jan 2026)
- IMF proposed 12-month emergency revenue mobilization plan
- Saudi Arabia announced $2 billion injection into Central Bank of Yemen (delayed)
- Yemen banks saw 24% increase in unified budget
- Foreign currency holdings expanded from $758 million to over $938 million
